Service Description

What is Firewall as a Service?

vBridge Firewall as a Service (FWaaS) is a Fortinet firewall offering, for the protection and security of customer hosted infrastructure and networks.

There are two options for of Firewall as a Service deployment:

  1. Fortinet Virtual Domain (VDOM). 
  2. Fortinet Virtual Machine (FortiVM).

The main differences between the two are summarised below:

  1. With the VDOM solution:
    1. The hardware platform is fully managed and maintained by vBridge. This includes the firewall operating system (FortiOS) being updated and patched.
    2. b. vBridge manage and maintain the licensing and support.
    3. High Availability is included in the service by utilising Cluster Active/Passive Hardware Failover managed by vBridge.
    4. Network and Security functions benefit through hardware offloading to FortiASIC processors.
    5. Customers have limited Administration access. For example, there is no access to network interface and routing configurations.
  2. With the VM solution
    1. The customer has full administrative control over the device.
    2. High Availability is provided by vBridge hosting platform and occurs automatically on hardware fault.
    3. vBridge provide license and support. Customer manages license installation and renewal.
    4. Network connectivity (add or remove a virtual network adapter) is managed via the MyCloudSpace portal.

You will be allocated and billed for the following.

    1. VDOM
      1. Small/Medium/Large.
      2. Optional CIR Internet Bandwidth and IP address allocations
    2. VM
      1. Cost of the size of the VM (CPU and RAM memory)
      2. License for appropriate size of FortiVM

The quotas are allocated per Service Instance. i.e. Internet bandwidth cannot be shared between multiple firewall instances.

Roles and Responsibilities

Roles and Responsibilities - VDOM

vBridge engineers are generally responsible for underlying hardware platform. This includes the physical equipment, operating systems on that equipment, and the configuration and management of the Fortinet administrative platform. vBridge also perform regular backups of the overall firewall configuration. The customer or reseller team is responsible for the Security of the protected networks, the management of the Security policies and profiles, reporting on the VDOM.

Responsibilities of the Service Provider (vBridge)

  • Monitor resources and provide tuning and advisement for FortiGate service provider issues
  • Engineer all layers of the Fortinet technology stack for high availability and compliance to vBridge policy
  • Apply patches and upgrades as recommended for the Firewall infrastructure
  • Respond to MAC requests for actions that end users cannot complete. E.g. network or system changes.
  • Monitor and tune the hardware and network environment as needed
  • Coordinate with vendors for licensing, maintenance and support requests
  • Perform medium-term capacity planning for the security infrastructure

Firewall Virtual Domains (VDOM) may be managed by the customer, or may be contracted to a vBridge reseller. In either case, the responsivities of the endpoint administrator are:

  • Serve as the primary trouble shooter for issues with the firewall.
  • Maintaining and managing the VDOM configuration. Including Security Policies and Profiles, Address objects, Traffic Shaping Policies, Intrusion Prevention, NAT, Web/DNS filters, DLP, WAF and Anti-Spam policies.
  • Manage all logging and retention requirements.
  • Manage and maintain all required encryption keys for VPN etc.
  • Ensure that all client-side software is correctly licensed and reported.

Roles and Responsibilities - FortiVM

vBridge engineers are generally responsible for underlying hosting platform. This includes the physical equipment, operating systems on that equipment, and the configuration and management of the Hypervisor platform. The customer or reseller team is responsible for the Management of the FortiVM, the Security of the protected networks, the management of the Security policies and profiles, reporting on the VDOM.

Responsibilities of the Service Provider (vBridge):

  • Engineer all layers of the VMware technology stack for high availability and compliance to vBridge policy
  • Respond to support requests for escalation issues.
  • Monitor and tune the physical server and network environment as needed
  • Coordinate with vendors for licensing, maintenance and support requests

FortiVM are be managed by the customer, or may be contracted to a vBridge reseller. In either case, the responsivities of the endpoint administrator are:

  • Serve as the primary trouble shooter for issues with the firewall.
  • Maintaining and managing the VDOM configuration. Including Security Policies and Profiles, Address objects, Traffic Shaping Policies, Intrusion Prevention, NAT, Web/DNS filters, DLP, WAF and Anti-Spam policies.
  • Manage all logging and retention requirements.
  • Manage and maintain all required encryption keys for VPN etc.
  • Patching and FortiOS upgrades on the FortiVM
  • Ensure that all software is correctly licensed and reported. License Keys are supplied by vBridge

Optional Service

vBridge are able to assist with configuration of questions on the Service. Any work done on the configuration side of customer devices is done on a time and materials and best effort basis.

Optional Service – Direct Connect Backup

vBridge are able to offer a VDOM to act as a routing gateway for customers with a direct cross connect or Dark Fibre Access Service (DFAS) access to vBridge. This allows high speed access to vBridge Cloud Connect Backup service by integrating with customers internal networking. The cross connect has limited access to the Cloud Connect Backup Service only and is fully managed by vBridge.
