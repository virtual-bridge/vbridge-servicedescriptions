Service Description

What is Scale Out NAS?

vBridge is a SMB/NFS/HDFS compatible storage location for the retention of Unstructured data files.

The platform is designed for unstructured data that is presented to customer clients and servers over a file-based protocol rather than block storage. The storage platform provides underlying erasure encoding and data protection, with the ability to replicate data to another geographically diverse location.

The customer must have private network connectivity to vBridge for access to the SMB/NFS/HDFS endpoints. Clients may reside on customer premises, while servers accessing the unstructured data endpoints must be Infrastructure as a Service hosted within the vBridge hosting platform.

You will be allocated and billed for a quota for use of the Service.

1) Total size (in TB) of the of the committed size of data in the storage environment.

The quotas are allocated per tenant and are limited by location. The unstructured data is not intended for small SME file server replacement. As such, there is a minimum commit on the platform of 10TB.

Ingress and Egress bandwidth for this service to and from client devices and servers within the vBridge data centre is included in the service price point.

Roles and Responsibilities

vBridge engineers are generally responsible for the Service Provider side or the environment. This includes the vBridge network, physical equipment, the operating systems on that equipment, and the configuration and management of the service provider side of the Isilon platform. The customer or reseller team is responsible for the management of the on-premise software (MS Active Directory, DNS), network IP Pool Allocation, Delegated DNS zone for “smart connect” and DNS sub domain management.

Responsibilities of the Service Provider (vBridge):

  • Monitor resources and provide tuning and advisement for Isilon Platform service provider issues
  • Engineer all layers of the Isilon technology stack for high availability and compliance to vBridge policy
  • Apply patches and upgrades as recommended for the storage infrastructure
  • Monitor and tune the physical NAS and network environment as needed
  • Manage permissions and security groups within the storage infrastructure
  • Coordinate with vendors for licensing, maintenance and support requests
  • Perform medium-term capacity planning

Client endpoints (SMB/NFS/HDFS) many be managed by the customer, or may be contracted to a vBridge reseller. In either case, the responsivities of the endpoint administrator are:

  • Serve as the primary trouble shooter for issues with an endpoint.
  • Maintaining and managing the client-side environment. This includes the physical equipment, the operating systems on that equipment, the software, licensing and the configuration of the client side.
  • Manage and maintain all client-side domain/directory.
  • Ensure that all client-side software is correctly licensed and reported.
